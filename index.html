Product Soft Innovation — AirBloom Modular Micro-Forests

Who uses it: cities, campuses, developers, community orgs
#1

Title Page

Product Urban Air Quality Green Space Modular Install

Potential Users: City governments, universities, property developers, and community organizations.

Visual Depiction (Concept)

A tall modular “micro-forest” tower covered in layered plant beds, with a seating ring around the base.

Narrative Description ▾

AirBloom Modular Micro-Forests are modular vertical structures that combine air filtration systems with dense plant ecosystems. Each structure functions as a compact “micro-forest” that can be installed in urban areas such as sidewalks, plazas, and campuses.

The system filters polluted air through layered plant beds and bio-filters while also providing shaded green spaces for pedestrians. Sensors monitor air quality and automatically adjust airflow and irrigation to optimize plant health and filtration performance.

Prior Works Collection (PWC) ▾

Context & Drivers

Cities face air pollution and limited access to nature. AirBloom targets both environmental quality and public well-being by embedding a functional “micro-forest” into high-traffic areas. This is outside my comfort zone because it requires thinking about environmental design and physical infrastructure—not just software.

Resources Required

Environmental sensors Filtration membranes Modular structural frame Irrigation + airflow control Plant ecosystems

Precedents (Patent/Trademark Search Targets)

Similar prior work includes (1) vertical garden / green wall systems used on building facades (often aesthetic-first), and (2) urban air-cleaning “living” installations such as moss-based air purification units (e.g., CityTree-style concepts), as well as (3) smart air-quality monitoring networks deployed in cities.

AirBloom’s novelty claim is the integration of modular micro-forest infrastructure + active bio-filtration airflow + sensor-controlled irrigation/ventilation into one deployable public-space unit, rather than a decorative wall or a standalone sensor.

Suggested search terms (Google Patents / USPTO): “modular vertical garden air filtration”, “biofilter tower air purifier”, “moss air purification street furniture”, “smart irrigation vertical garden sensor”.

Process Soft Innovation — Adaptive Community Planning System (ACPS)

A participatory planning workflow that converts resident input into actionable planning insights
#2

Title Page

Process Participation Transparency Decision Support

Potential Users: City planners, urban development agencies, and local communities.

Visual Depiction (Concept)

A platform where residents vote/comment on proposals; planners receive synthesized insights.

Narrative Description ▾

The Adaptive Community Planning System is a digital platform that allows communities to participate directly in urban planning decisions. Residents submit ideas, vote on design proposals, and provide feedback through an interactive interface.

The system analyzes feedback and generates planning insights for officials, helping them prioritize infrastructure changes that provide the greatest community benefit while improving transparency and trust.

Prior Works Collection (PWC) ▾

Context & Drivers

Traditional planning often relies on limited public meetings and fragmented surveys, which can miss broad community input. ACPS creates a continuous, structured participation loop.

Resources Required

Web/mobile platform Secure voting + identity checks Analytics + summarization Community outreach

Precedents (Patent/Trademark Search Targets)

Similar prior work includes online community engagement portals that collect comments/surveys for civic projects, and participatory budgeting / digital public consultation systems that let residents vote on proposals.

ACPS’s novelty claim is a full workflow that combines continuous participation + structured analysis + translation into prioritized planning outputs (instead of one-off surveys or unstructured comment boards).

Suggested search terms (Google Patents / USPTO): “community engagement platform city planning voting”, “participatory budgeting software system”, “public consultation portal analytics”, “citizen feedback analysis urban planning”.

Service Soft Innovation — Neighborhood Resource Navigator

A guided service to help residents access local essentials quickly and confidently
#3

Title Page

Service Access Guidance Kiosks + App

Potential Users: Residents in underserved communities.

Visual Depiction (Concept)

Interactive kiosks in public spaces + a companion mobile app for step-by-step directions.

Narrative Description ▾

The Neighborhood Resource Navigator is a service that helps individuals access essential services within their local area—food banks, healthcare clinics, job training programs, and educational opportunities.

Users interact through a mobile app or public kiosks. The system identifies nearby resources and provides step-by-step guidance on how to access them, reducing barriers caused by fragmented information.

Prior Works Collection (PWC) ▾

Context & Drivers

Many residents struggle to locate and navigate available services. Information is scattered across organizations, creating friction and missed opportunities for help.

Resources Required

App + kiosk software Local org partnerships Updated services database Wayfinding + accessibility design

Precedents (Patent/Trademark Search Targets)

Similar prior work includes community resource directories (e.g., “211”-style service listings) and social service locator apps that help users find nearby assistance programs. Some systems also use public information kiosks for wayfinding or civic services.

The Navigator’s novelty claim is shifting from a static directory into a guided experience: kiosk access + step-by-step instructions (what to bring, eligibility, next steps) + a unified interface that reduces friction for users who may not have reliable devices or time.

Suggested search terms (Google Patents / USPTO): “public kiosk social services locator”, “resource directory guided assistance system”, “service navigation eligibility workflow app”, “community resource kiosk software”.

Experience Soft Innovation — Future City Immersion Lab

An immersive experience to help the public “walk through” proposed city changes
#4

Title Page

Experience Immersive Visualization Collaborative Design

Potential Users: Students, urban planners, and community members.

Visual Depiction (Concept)

A projection room where participants explore future city scenarios and adjust design elements in real time.

Narrative Description ▾

The Future City Immersion Lab is an interactive experience where participants explore possible future versions of their city. Using immersive environments and simulations, participants walk through digital representations of proposed urban designs.

Users can interact with the environment, modify elements, and immediately see how different decisions would change their neighborhoods—making planning more understandable and participatory.

Prior Works Collection (PWC) ▾

Context & Drivers

Planning proposals are hard to interpret when presented as technical drawings. This experience helps the public understand and discuss real tradeoffs before projects are built.

Resources Required

Simulation software Projection/immersive hardware Urban modeling data Interactive interfaces

Precedents (Patent/Trademark Search Targets)

Similar prior work includes VR/AR architectural walkthrough systems and urban simulation / visualization tools used by designers to preview built environments. Immersive projection rooms and interactive exhibits also exist in museums and design labs.

The Immersion Lab’s novelty claim is a civic-focused experience: public-facing, collaborative, and tied to planning decisions with “what-if” controls (green space, traffic flow, density) so non-experts can understand impacts and participate meaningfully.

Suggested search terms (Google Patents / USPTO): “immersive projection room urban planning”, “virtual reality city planning public engagement”, “interactive urban simulation exhibit”, “collaborative design visualization system”.
